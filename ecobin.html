How can we educate Canadians on the benefits of recycling properly and reduce the economic impact of contaminated recyclables?

Discovery of the Problem

I introduced the problem of garbage sorting from my own daily struggle to sort out pieces of garbage after I have purchased products

Pain Points

Accessibility of a sorting method
Frustration of spending more time needed
Motivation to keep the habit of using the app

Our Solution

A user friendly, minimalisticGarbage sorting app using your camera to sort (through machine learning) or location for manual input.



Why this Solution?

  • • The phone is accessible to a wide range of people in Canada.
  • • Through constant use, it will educate them.
  • • The gamification allows them to stay motivated

Our other solution was to have a device that sorts it itself by the garbage bin. However, it could be expensive and not all places will be willing to implement it.

Features

  • Camera Sorting

    Using the users location, the app will help the user find there trash they need help sorting quicker, by finding the stores, fast food and the trash they generate

  • Trash Geolocation

    Using the users location, the app will help the user find there trash they need help sorting quicker, by finding the stores, fast food and the trash they generate

  • Gamification

    Gamifying the experience allows users to stay more motivated. They earn RBC Award points with each level they gain. The amount and level need to be determined by testing.

  • Data Collection

    Data collected may be sold back to local businesses for further education on sustainability
