Ecobin: RBC AmpHack

Minimalistic, Sustainable App that promotes & makes sorting garbage
into different binds,simple by using your camera or location to sort it for you.

View Mockup

Technical Skills Used:
XD, HTML, CSS

  • PROCESS

Description & Concept

Minimalistic, Vibrant, Fast & sustainable App that promotes & makes sorting garbage into different bins, simple by using your camera (through machine learning) or location to sort it for you. It's meant to be used daily. Users will find motivation to use the app through the gamification portion of the app. Users are able to earn RBC award points with the use of the camera and providing information about their trash.

Solution won 1st place at RBC Amplify Hackathon

Design & UI

We wanted to intervene less on a persons time by having only four main pages or functions. This created a fast & Simple experience using hierarchy of importance, affordances, and a simple UI. Shortening the amount of time on the go. In reality, I would have liked to do user testing to see the amount of time spent. Because we went for a clean white look, a hint of vibrancy of green and blue (representing the earth naturally) kept it visually appealing and a tool to direct the user to a certain location on the page.

Features of the App

Camera Sorting
Through machine learning, the user can take a picture with an app and receives which bin it belongs in.

Trash Geolocation
Using the users location, the app will help the user find there trash they need help sorting quicker, by finding the stores, fast food and the trash they generate.

Gamification
Gamifying the experience allows users to stay more motivated. They earn RBC Award points with each level they gain. The amount and level need to be determined by testing.

Data collection
Data collected may be sold back to local businesses for further education on sustainability

Discovery of the Problem

The question for this hackathon revolved on how we may better Canadian lives. I introduced a problem to my team about my frustration of sorting garbage when I purchase something from a store, or in general, sorting trash. It was a human and environmental problem that is costing Canada millions and will even more in the future. It isn't that Canadians don't want to help the environment. The problem is that it's not made very easy. Especially with each place having different garbage sorting methods.

My Role & Teammates

My role was as a UI/UX Designer. However, I also coded the front-end aspect, worked on the presentation portion, presented along side with my teammates & did Q&A. My teammates were a business analyst, data scientist and a developer. Because of my education in interaction design, I learned how to communicate between the three different fields despite knowing only small portions. We worked successfully and collaboratively in each portion of the work.
